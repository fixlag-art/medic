Revise

Rules reset, routines re-shaped

日本語
Author: Hatchy ／ Narrator: Roku

Revise—recovery isn’t linear; we learn our way back.

Rewrite the baselines

Redraw the line between “can” and “cannot” to fit reality. Yesterday’s normal may not suit today’s self. Encode the new baselines into calendars and checklists.

Pacing

Allocate load by week and block recovery days first. Do the must-do tasks in the morning when the battery is high; leave thicker margins in the afternoon.

Nutrition, sleep, movement

Keep the triad first. Protein and hydration; a fixed wind-down ritual for sleep; gentle aerobic work and stretches for movement. Small deposits build the foundation.

Reconnecting

Share the “current spec” of yourself: slower replies, shorter meetings, requests for escort. When rules are spoken and agreed, awkwardness shrinks.

Preparing for recurrence

Update the onset playbook: contacts, meds list, hospitals, transport. Readiness for the first minutes protects the coming weeks.

Why I write

Revise is not restoration—it is re-creation of the best we can do now. Writing the process becomes support for tomorrow’s self.

Note: Personal narrative, not medical advice. If symptoms arise, consult clinicians promptly.
